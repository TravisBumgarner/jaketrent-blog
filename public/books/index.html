Books

  • To the Rescue

    Heidi Swinton

    This is the biography of Thomas Monson, President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. Reading the account of his life was super inspiring, making me want to be a better person every day.

  • Decisive: How to Make Better Choices in Life and Work

    Chip & Dan Heath

    There are specific traits of being human that make us not natural at making great decisions. Recognizing these weaknesses and pitfalls and learning new skills can help when important decisions come.

  • Warren Buffet’s Management Secrets

    Mary Buffet & David Clark

    It turns out Warren Buffet has some real people and management skills behind his sprawling financial success. These are down-to-earth and useful insights from a man worth learning some principles from.

  • Who Moved My Cheese?

    Spencer Johnson

    An allegorical story about mice and little people in a maze of life that can help prompt helpful thinking about what is worth pursuing in life. Life changes – so does the location of the cheese. Move with the cheese. What would you do if you had no fear?

  • Linchpin: Are You Indispensable?

    Seth Godin

    The world has changed. We each have an inner genius, and we need to use it. Artists provide real value, blazing trails without a map and shipping their art, again and again.

  • The Real Crash

    Peter Schiff

    Peter gives his view of the past economic bubbles and his predicted current bubble and future pop. He outlines clearyl many economic principles, his opinions, and offers potential solutions.

  • Bonhoeffer: Pastor, Martyr, Prophet, Spy

    Eric Metaxas

    Bonhoeffer was not well-known to me but was definitely revealed in this book as a singular person. He is known for taking a strong stance for Christianity and conspiring against Hitler in WWII.

  • Switch: How to Change Things When Change is Hard

    Chip Heath & Dan Heath

    Likening people to an elephant and rider, Chip and Dan show a lot of interesting examples of how people and situations have been successfully changed for the better.

    My Review

  • Outliers: The Story of Success

    Malcolm Gladwell

    Malcolm argues that we over-idolize the self-made made. He focuses on stories of people where success predictably includes unique opportunity and cultural background.

  • Well-being 2.0

    Tom Rath

    The facets of our life are interconnected. We cannot have independent wellness in one and avoid the other. For greatest wellbeing, we should consider our total wellness.

  • Developing an Ember Edge

    Jamie White et al

    A great survey of the language API similar to the official Ember guides. It also includes half a book of the implementation of a real project.

    My Review

  • Start: Punch Fear in the Face, Escape Average and Do Work That Matters

    Jon Acuff

    We have great opportunities to begin something and start towards mastery. Often the largest barrier is to start. So, get movin’!

    My Review

  • So Good They Can’t Ignore You

    Cal Newport

    Some tell you to follow your passions. Cal tells you to master what you do, opportunities and passion will follow.

    My Review

  • Smashing Node

    Guillermo Rauch

    Node.js is hot. This book is green. Read it. It’s a survey of Node and a variety of interesting features.

    My Review

  • Quitter

    Jon Acuff

    Quit your day job at the right moment in order to pursue your dream. At the right moment, the transition can be much smoother and your dream is more likely to be realized.

    My Review

  • The Lean Startup

    Eric Ries

    Learn to bootstrap your idea in the form of a minimum viable product. Create your feedback loop, and make your product better through your learning. If you aren’t embarrassed when you release it, you’ve waited too long.

  • Platform: Get Noticed in a Noisy World

    Michael Hyatt

    To build your platform, you’ll need an online presence. Michael is a social media mezzo forte.

    My Review

  • Drive: The Surprising Truth About What Motivates Us

    Daniel Pink

    People are motivated by various things. It might insightful to read this and figure out what motivates you.

  • A Whole New Mind: Why Right-brainers Will Rule the Future

    Daniel Pink

    Abundance, automation, and Asia have created a new world. In this world, inventors who can create and synthesize ideas into being will become more and more valuable.

    My Review

  • Leadership and Self-Deception

    The Arbinger Institute

    The people that you interact with are just that – people. Things will go better and we’ll all be happier as we learn to treat people like people and not objects.

  • The Total Money Makeover

    Dave Ramsey

    Dave went broke and vowed it would never happen to him again. He learned valuable principles in handling money and shares them with his intense personality and clear baby steps. So good.

  • Release It!: Design and Deploy Production-Ready Software

    Michael Nygard

    Releasing software doesn’t just mean meeting the product feature requirements. It means meeting certain non-functional requirements that make the app work and your life better must also be met.

    My Review
